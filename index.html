Painting cost depends on

Height of the building

Cost is directly proportional to the elevation (height), usually measured in square feet/metres. Primarily this type of cost applies to exterior painting of landed property or for a Condominium with extremely high ceilings.

Size of Painting Area

Cost is directly proportional to the painting surface area, usually measured in square feet/metres.

Complexity

When you opt to add more colours, more effort is required to set up & complete the painting work.

Type of Paint used

Each paint type has a particular purpose and will give a different look (after finishing). Example Nippons' Matex paint is cheap/litre compared to Nippons' "All in one" paint/litre.

When you paint your residential properties, use 60% light, 30% medium, and 10% dark colours. This colouring equation will give your home an even-toned look and feel.

Find out the types of paints that are used to paint the residential properties

Antifungal / Anti Mould Paints

Antifungal paints protect your home from fungus formation and mold formation. Usually, preferred application spots are areas that attract moisture, for example, bathrooms.

Health Specific Paints

Virus guard paints provide antibacterial properties. Mozzieguard (Nippon Paints) protects homes from dengue-causing mosquitoes. Odourless paints, as the name suggests, they are odourless.

Oil-based and Water-based House Paint

Oil-based paints are made with oil, and water-based paint with water. Oil-based paints are more durable, shiny, and take longer to dry, and water-based paints are thinner, matte, and take less time to dry. Oil-based paints are used for doors, door frames, and furniture, whereas water-based paints are for walls, ceilings, etc.

Interior and Exterior House Paint

Most paints are labelled interior or exterior. Interior paints are usually water-based, and exterior paints are typically oil-based.
