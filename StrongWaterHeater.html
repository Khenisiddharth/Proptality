We install Different Types of Water Heater

We install Different Types of Water Heater
Electric Storage Water Heaters

Electric storage water comes with an inbuilt tank to store the water. When you turn on the power, it will start heating the water on the tank, and it will re-heat the water when it’s not been used for a long time when you start using the hot water. It will release the heated water through one outlet and allow cold water to the storage tank to heat it for consistent temperature delivery and to ensure that the tank is always full.

Gas Storage Water Heaters

Gas water heaters are not commonly used because it requires a consistent gas supply to heat up the water. Gas water heaters are more expensive than electric water heaters. Also, filling the gas is a bit difficult. But it can heat the water 2X faster than electrical water heaters.

Digital Storage Water Heaters

Digital storage water heaters come with a thermostat panel and a display to indicate the temperature. The primary use of this type of storage water heater is you can set the temperature according to your wish, and it will deliver the heated consistent temperature water for a great bath. This type of water heater is predominantly used in landed properties & commercial buildings.

Solar Storage Water Heaters

Solar storage water heaters are usually placed on the roof of buildings to receive solar energy directly from the sun. Once the power is stored, it is converted into electricity to heat the water. So it does not consume any electricity.

Factors to consider before buying a water heater?

Factors to consider before buying a water heater?
1. Water Pressure

When you purchase the water heater, check whether the heater is capable of maintaining consistent water pressure throughout the bath cycle.

2. Consistent Hot Water

Storage water heaters can deliver hot water at a consistent temperature because the tank is preheated already. So there is no drop in hot water temperature.

3. Compatibility

Storage water heaters can accompany any type of shower fixture, but instant water heaters are suitable for only hand showers.

4. Maintenance

You have to opt for annual maintenance to increase the durability of our storage water heater. But it is definitely worth it.

5. Cost

The installation cost of a storage water heater is high as it involves multipoint connections installation and piping.

6. Energy Saving

Storage water heaters consume more energy to heat the water and the tank for a consistent temperature.

7. Space Requirements

Usually, storage water heaters occupy larger spaces when compared with instant heaters because they have to serve multiple bathrooms simultaneously.

8. Family Size

Choose your water heater based on your family members. Storage water heaters can help more people at the same time.

Maintenance Checklist for Water Heaters

Maintenance Checklist for Water Heaters
1. Placement of water heaters

When installing the water heater, make sure the heater is accessible for plumbing or maintenance-related works in the future.

2. Determine water heater type

Choose the right water heater based on your needs. Also, note the water heater’s product number, serial code, water heater capacity, and date of purchase.

3. Make sure the area surrounding your water heater is clear

Always ensure that the installed water heater is viewable to notice whether the pipe is leaking or any other issues.

4. Draining and flushing your water heater

Draining and flushing of the water heater must be performed once a year to do yearly maintenance to keep up the water heater's performance.

5. Yearly inspection of your water heater

Yes, you must perform an inspection or audit once a year to avoid huge damages and promptly address the issues.

6. Valves that regulate pressure

Check the values and piping system as it delivers high-temperature water. So get help from the licensed plumber to ensure the water heater's performance.

7. Water softeners

Use water softeners to avoid the corrosion inside the water heater and to remove the residue from the hot water deposits.
